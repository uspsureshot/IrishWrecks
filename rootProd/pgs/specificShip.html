S.F.V. City of Bristol

Off the coast of the city of Ballintoy, this Fleetwood Steam Trawler sank on October 22nd, 1906.

S.F.V. City of Bristol

Diving Information:

Lies in Larrybone Bay. Hull plating starting to fall off its framework. Inshore, the tide is negligible, but further off-shore the current can run up to 4 knots.

Historical Information:

Built in 1903 by the Goole Shipbuilding Co., Goole, she grossed 214 tons and measured 35.05 x 6.40 x 3.50m. Powered by a 3 cylinder triple-expansion steam engine.

Owner was T.F. Kelsall, Fleetwood.

Stranded and wrecked in a storm between Sheep Island and the Mainland.
