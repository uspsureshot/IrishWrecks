Terminology

...A landlubber's guide to nautical talk.

Words commonly used on this website that may be unfamiliar to novices and landlubbers.

Bombed by U-Boat:

Bombs or Scuttling Charges placed on-board by U-Boat's crew, after vessel's crew have been allowed to abandon ship.

Dimensions:

Imperial Feet and Inches unless otherwise stated.

Distances:

Given in Imperial measurement only.

Foundered:

Filled with water and sank.

Latitude/Longitude:

Accurate marks have "GPS" in brackets beside Lat/Long., otherwise they have been calculated from an Admiralty Chart by survivors of the wreck, or by divers who have visited the site.

"OFF" & "NEAR":

Position unknown but within a few miles of the Location the vessel is listed under.

Origin of vessel:

Origin is that of the vessel's Port of Registry e.g. "London Barque".

Sail Vessel construction:

Wooden construction unless otherwise stated.

Shelled by U-Boat:

Vessel sunk by gunfire from U-Boat with or without vessel's crew escaping beforehand.

Steamer construction:

Steel unless otherwise stated.

Tonnage:

No distinction has been made between gross or net tonnage (Imperial).

Went/Drove/Ran ashore or stranded:

Struck the shore/beach/sand bank while still under power/sail. In most cases the vessel probably was salvaged unless stated that it was "wrecked" once aground.
